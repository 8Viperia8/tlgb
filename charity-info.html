Stichting INGKA Foundation

This charitable foundation was established in the Netherlands and holds assets worth $36 billion. Founded by Ingvar Kamprad, the founder of IKEA, it focuses on supporting young talents in the arts, architecture, and design. The foundation also collaborates with UN programs to address global challenges such as refugee resettlement and youth development.

Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation

Founded in 2000 by Bill and Melinda Gates, this foundation is one of the largest private charitable organizations in the world, with assets exceeding $50 billion. It focuses on reducing global poverty, improving healthcare, and enhancing education. The foundation supports initiatives in underdeveloped countries, including vaccination programs, agricultural development, and access to clean water.

Wellcome Trust

Established in 1936 in the UK, Wellcome Trust is a global leader in funding medical research. With assets worth tens of billions of euros, it supports groundbreaking studies in genetics, infectious diseases, and public health. The trust played a pivotal role in the Human Genome Project and continues to fund innovative research worldwide.

Ford Foundation

Founded in 1936 by Edsel Ford, the son of Henry Ford, this foundation promotes social justice, economic equality, and cultural preservation. Operating globally, it funds projects that empower marginalized communities, support education, and address issues like climate change and human rights. The foundation has a strong presence in developing countries.

DAFNE

DAFNE (Donors and Foundations Networks in Europe) is a collaborative network of 22 organizations across Europe. It aims to strengthen the philanthropic sector by promoting best practices, advocacy, and collaboration among foundations. DAFNE's efforts focus on improving living standards, fostering innovation, and ensuring transparency in charitable work.

United Way Worldwide

Operating since 1887, United Way Worldwide is a global nonprofit organization present in 46 countries. It mobilizes over 10 million donors annually to support healthcare, education, and financial stability for vulnerable populations. With over 3 million volunteers, the organization addresses critical issues such as poverty, drug abuse, and disaster relief.

Howard Hughes Medical Institute

Founded in 1953, HHMI is one of the largest private research institutions in the United States. It funds cutting-edge biomedical research and supports young scientists who have made significant contributions to science. To date, researchers funded by HHMI have won over 24 Nobel Prizes, highlighting its impact on global science.

Robert Bosch Foundation

Established after the death of Robert Bosch, this German foundation focuses on advancing medicine, education, and science. It supports private educational institutions, research centers, and hospitals worldwide. The foundation also provides grants for students and researchers, fostering innovation and academic excellence.

UNICEF

UNICEF (United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund) operates globally to improve the lives of children and families in need. Supported by the United Nations, it provides humanitarian aid during emergencies, advocates for children's rights, and works to ensure access to healthcare, education, and clean water. UNICEF collaborates with governments, NGOs, and celebrities to amplify its impact.
